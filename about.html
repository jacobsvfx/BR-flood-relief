About Us

BRFloodRelief is dedicated to supporting communities in Brazil affected by flooding. Our mission is to provide resources, guidance, and real-time updates to help individuals and families stay safe and healthy. We collaborate with local officials and international organizations to maximize our impact and reach.

The World Health Organization (WHO) is a specialized agency of the United Nations that coordinates responses to international public health issues and emergencies, dedicated to raising public awareness of disaster prevention, health, and safety. In response to the leptospirosis outbreak posed by the 2024 floods in the Rio Grande do Sul state in Brazil, we have established this website with the intention to aid in the recovery of the area, and the prevention of future infectious disease incidents.

Our Work

Flood Relief Efforts

Our mission involves providing a means of relief and aid to those affected by the flooding in the Rio Grande do Sul state in Brazil in 2024. We aim to create a system to prevent another outbreak of the bacterial disease Leptospirosis in the case of more flooding. This platform involves multiple aspects:

Health and Safety Campaigns

First, create and map easily accessible “flood stations” that provide people with waterproof clothing and essentials such as food and water to prevent contact with contaminated water.

Community Engagement

Second, create a platform to inform the public of the dangers and risks of flood-associated resistant Leptospirosis. This website includes a donation page, an information section, and a means for locals to contact doctors and other professionals.
